Mithril is a single REST API (and a hosted MCP server) for trading **Kalshi**
and **Polymarket** — both live today. You attach your own exchange credentials
to a subaccount; Mithril signs your orders, works them into thin books under a
slippage cap, checks every order against server-side risk limits, and gives you
one unified view of positions, fills, and P&L across venues.

The goal is simple: **you spend your time on alpha, and Mithril handles the
plumbing** — venue auth, order execution, guardrails, and risk accounting.

## Why teams use Mithril

<Steps>
  <Step title="One API, both venues">
    Kalshi's RSA request signing and Polymarket's EIP-712 wallet signing,
    allowances, and proxy wallets are handled server-side. You send JSON to one
    base URL; a unified `mkt_` identifier maps the same event across venues.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Smart execution">
    Preview an order's impact against live depth, then work it with
    `smart_taker`, `iceberg`, `peg`, or `adaptive` strategies under a slippage
    cap. Every fill gets an execution receipt: arrival mid, achieved average
    price, what a naive sweep would have cost, and fees per venue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Risk on every order">
    Notional caps, per-market limits, concentration, max daily loss, and a kill
    switch — enforced before the venue ever sees the order. Isolate strategies
    with subaccounts, each with its own credentials and limits.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keys stay yours">
    Your Kalshi API key or Polymarket wallet key is envelope-encrypted (X25519)
    and decrypted only in memory to sign an order. Never logged, never returned.
    Mithril never takes custody of funds.
  </Step>
</CardGroup>
</Steps>
